# Leaders, Gainers and Unexpected Winners in the Enterprise AI Arms Race
**Authors:** Sarah Wang, Justin Kahl, Shangda Xu

**Category:** Strategy & Governance

**Published:** 30 January 2026

## Key Points
- OpenAI leads enterprise AI with 78% production adoption, but Anthropic grew 25% since May 2025 and Google Gemini is also gaining market share rapidly.
- Use case leadership varies significantly: OpenAI dominates chatbots and knowledge management, Anthropic leads in software development and data analysis, while Gemini performs strongly across most categories except coding.
- Microsoft remains dominant in enterprise AI applications through M365 Copilot and GitHub Copilot, with 65% of enterprises preferring incumbent solutions despite startup narratives.
- Trust in closed-source frontier labs has increased substantially, with approximately 80% of enterprises now comfortable hosting models directly with providers rather than through CSPs.
- Enterprise AI spend is expected to grow approximately 65% in 2026, rising from an average of ~$7M to ~$11.6M per enterprise.

## Summary
This article presents findings from a16z's third annual CIO survey of 100 Global 2000 companies examining enterprise AI adoption trends. OpenAI maintains the leadership position with 78% of enterprises using its models in production, but the competitive landscape is shifting. Anthropic posted the largest share increase since May 2025, growing 25% in enterprise penetration to reach 44% production usage, while Google Gemini also shows strong gains.

The survey reveals that leadership depends heavily on use case. OpenAI dominates early, horizontal applications like chatbots and knowledge management, while Anthropic leads in software development and data analysis - areas where CIOs cite rapid capability gains since late 2024 as the catalyst. Microsoft remains the dominant force in enterprise AI applications, with M365 Copilot leading enterprise chat and GitHub Copilot maintaining coding leadership. Notably, 65% of enterprises prefer incumbent solutions, citing trust, integration with existing systems, and procurement simplicity.

Enterprise trust in closed-source frontier models has increased substantially, with over a third now preferring them due to model quality improvements, limited internal AI talent, and data security considerations. Enterprise AI spending continues to outpace expectations, rising from ~$4.5M to ~$7M over two years, with projected growth of approximately 65% in 2026. The survey also notes that reported ROI remains less dramatic than online discourse suggests, indicating enterprises are still learning how to deploy AI effectively and often need partners to translate models into real workflows.
